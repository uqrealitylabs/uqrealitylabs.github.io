# Code of Conduct

UQRL is committed to a safe, respectful, and productive project environment. This Code of Conduct applies to all UQRL project spaces, including repositories, issues, pull requests, meetings, workshops, demos, online channels, and industry project activities.

## Expected Behaviour

Participants must act professionally, respect others, follow lawful and university requirements, and protect private project information. Disagreement is allowed; harassment, intimidation, discrimination, retaliation, and bad-faith disruption are not.

## Unacceptable Behaviour

Unacceptable behaviour includes, but is not limited to:

- offensive comments or conduct related to gender, gender identity or expression, sexual orientation, disability, physical appearance, race, religion, age, nationality, socioeconomic status, education, programming experience, or technical background
- sexual images, sexual language, or unwelcome sexual attention in project spaces
- deliberate intimidation, stalking, doxxing, or sustained disruption
- harassing photography, recording, screenshots, or publication of private communications
- unwelcome physical contact
- threats, calls for violence, incitement to violence, or encouragement of self-harm
- disclosure of personal information, credentials, private keys, client material, paid assets, licence files, or confidential project information
- plagiarism, misrepresentation of authorship, or knowingly submitting work that breaches licence or academic integrity obligations
- unsolicited advertising or recruitment in UQRL-managed spaces without permission

Participants asked to stop unacceptable behaviour must comply immediately.

## AI-Assisted Work and Vibecoding

AI tools may be used where allowed by the project, course, client, and licence terms. The person submitting the work remains responsible for it.

Do not submit AI-generated or vibecoded changes that you cannot explain, test, maintain, or licence. Do not paste private code, client material, credentials, unreleased assets, student data, or confidential logs into public AI tools. Pull requests using substantial AI assistance should say so when it affects review, authorship, licensing, security, or maintainability.

Reviewers may reject code that appears unreviewed, untested, insecure, unlicensed, undocumented, or inconsistent with the project.

## Industry Project Authority

For industry projects, task allocation, priority, scope, access, reviewer assignment, and delivery decisions are made by the workshop director or their delegate. Participants must not reassign work, change client scope, publish project material, or contact external stakeholders on behalf of the project without approval.

## Reporting and Enforcement

If you experience or witness unacceptable behaviour, contact the workshop director, project lead, UQRL organiser, or relevant university channel as soon as practical. Reports should be handled with discretion and shared only with people who need to assess or resolve the matter.

UQRL may take action it considers appropriate, including warnings, removal from project spaces, reassignment of work, loss of repository access, removal from an industry project, referral to university processes, or referral to law enforcement where appropriate.

## University Policy

Participants are expected to comply with applicable University of Queensland policies, including student integrity, misconduct, workplace health and safety, privacy, and information security requirements.

This Code of Conduct is adapted from community anti-harassment policies, including the UQCS and Typelevel Codes of Conduct.
